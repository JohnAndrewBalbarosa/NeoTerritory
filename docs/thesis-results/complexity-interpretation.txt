Overall verdict for the panel: the F1 spread is not a bug in the analyzer — it is the COHORT signature. High-familiarity patterns over-trigger because participants reach for them, low-familiarity patterns under-trigger because participants do not name them. The analyzer ground truth (what was actually in the code) is the constant; the human review is the variable. The expected-norm projection in the admin dashboard quantifies this baseline so the actual numbers can be checked against the documented assumption rather than against a uniform "every pattern should score the same" yardstick.
