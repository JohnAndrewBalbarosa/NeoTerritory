QUESTION:
Two classes each wrap a held member and forward calls to it. Why is this evidence insufficient to label one Adapter and the other Decorator?

OPTIONS:
A. Because forwarding is illegal in C++
B. Because wrapping always means Adapter

A Prototype subclass owns a pointer to a heap buffer, and its clone() performs only a member-wise (shallow) copy. Is this a sound clone, and why?
