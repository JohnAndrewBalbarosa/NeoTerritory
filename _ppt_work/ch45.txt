[0070|] Cronbach’s Alpha
[0071|] Cronbach's alpha is a measure of how well the items in a scale measure the same underlying construct. A high Cronbach's alpha indicates that the items on the scale are measuring the same underlying construct, and that the scores on the scale are consistent. In this study, Cronbach's alpha was calculated for each of the six subscales of the survey. The results showed that all six subscales had high Cronbach's alphas, ranging from 0.755 to 0.871. This indicates that the survey is a reliable measure of software quality.

[0138|] CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
[0139|] The thesis focuses on addressing the research objectives and answering key questions related to the blood bank system. The primary objective of the study is to improve the understanding and effectiveness of the blood bank system and mitigate the adverse effects of blood loss. The chapter highlights five key questions that contribute to the quality and outcomes of the research.
[0140|] The first question focuses on determining the essential data required by the system to provide accurate information about blood inventory details, including blood type, lifespan, and availability. This data is crucial for improving the accessibility and reliability of blood supplies.
[0141|] The second question addresses the development of an effective organizational framework within the application to manage large amounts of data. This includes organizing information about available blood packs, their specific details, and maintaining a database of reserved donors and their contact information. Such organization facilitates efficient tracking and allocation of blood resources.
[0142|] Lastly, the study emphasizes the significance of evaluating the system's quality in terms of functionality, efficiency, usability, reliability, and maintainability. The application of standards such as ISO 25010 and Cronbach's alpha enables the assessment and enhancement of the overall performance and robustness of the application.
[0143|] By addressing these research objectives and questions, the study aims to provide practical contributions to the field of computer science and offer valuable insights for practitioners, researchers, patients, blood donors, and healthcare organizations involved in the blood bank system. The findings and recommendations presented in this chapter contribute to enhancing the overall understanding, effectiveness, and efficiency of the blood bank system, ultimately improving patient outcomes and saving lives.
[0144|] 5.1 Conclusion
[0145|] This chapter presents a summary of findings based on the collected data from the participants. The objective of this research was to evaluate the various aspects of the BloodLine web application, including functional stability, maintainability, efficiency, usability, reliability, and security.
[0146|] The findings indicate that the participants strongly agree with the different categories of the BloodLine web application. The functional stability category received the highest weighted mean of 3.572, indicating a strong agreement among the participants. This suggests that the web application performs its intended functions effectively and reliably.
[0147|] Maintainability, with a weighted mean of 3.444, also garnered strong agreement from the participants. This implies that the web application is perceived as easily maintainable and scalable, allowing for future updates and improvements.
[0148|] In terms of efficiency, the participants strongly agreed, as evidenced by a weighted mean of 3.533. This suggests that BloodLine performs tasks efficiently and optimizes its resources effectively.
[0149|] The usability category received a weighted mean of 3.511, indicating that participants found the web application to be intuitive, interactive, and user-friendly. This suggests that users can easily navigate and interact with the features of BloodLine.
[0150|] Overall, the total average of 3.531 indicates a strong agreement among the participants in all aspects of the BloodLine web application. These findings highlight the positive perception of users towards the functional stability, maintainability, efficiency, usability, reliability, and security of BloodLine.
[0151|] These findings contribute to the validation and evaluation of the BloodLine web application, supporting its potential for real-world application and reinforcing its value as a reliable and user-friendly tool for accessing blood bank services.
[0152|] The results of this study provide valuable insights for further enhancements and improvements to the BloodLine web application, ensuring that it continues to meet the needs and expectations of its users in terms of functionality, maintainability, efficiency, usability, reliability, and security.
[0153|] 5.2 Recommendation
[0154|] The Following recommendations are offered based on the work accomplished during this project:
[0155|] Integration with existing Electronic Health Records (EHR): Consider exploring the integration of the blood bank system with existing electronic health record systems. This integration would allow for seamless information exchange between healthcare facilities, enabling real-time access to patient data, blood type requirements, and availability. By incorporating EHR integration, the efficiency and accuracy of the blood bank system can be further enhanced.
[0156|] Collaboration with Blood Banks and Healthcare Organizations: One important recommendation we would like to emphasize is the inclusion of a client in the research process. In our study, we opted for a general-use approach and did not specifically explore the perspective and input of a client. The involvement of a client ensures that the research outcomes are directly aligned with their needs and can be effectively implemented in real-world scenarios. Collaborating with a client promotes a user-centered approach and fosters a deeper understanding of the practical implications of the research findings. Therefore, we highly recommend incorporating the client perspective into future studies to maximize the impact and applicability of the research.
